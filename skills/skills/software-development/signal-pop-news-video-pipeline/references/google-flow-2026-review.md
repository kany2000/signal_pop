# Google Flow Review 2026 — Free AI Video Studio

Source: aivideopicks.com review + google blog + labs.google/fx/tools/flow

## Access
- URL: https://labs.google/fx/tools/flow
- Sign in: any Google account (free)
- Also at: flow.google.com (redirects)

## Free Tier (May 2026)
- 100 starter credits (one-time)
- 50 daily credits (resets every 24h)
- No watermark on free-tier output
- Commercial use allowed (check Google ToS)

## Models Available in Flow
- **Veo 3.1** — video gen with native audio, dialogue, lip-sync, SFX, music
- **Nano Banana** (a.k.a. "Nano Banana Pro" in Chinese marketing) — image gen, subject consistency, text rendering
- **Gemini Omni** — multimodal input, video editing from any reference, conversational editing
- **Imagen 4** — merged into Flow in Feb 2026 (formerly ImageFX + Whisk)

## Video Specs
- Native resolution: 720p
- Free upscale to: 1080p
- Max per generation: 4–8 seconds
- Extend via chaining: +7s hops, up to 20 hops (~148s total)
- Quality degrades after 3–5 hops
- Native audio: environmental sounds, dialogue with lip-sync, background music
- Max supported: 4K @ 60fps (Ultra tier, via upscale)

## Known Limitations
- 8-second clip limit per generation (mitigated by Extend)
- No custom audio upload
- No API access — web UI only
- Character consistency across multiple clips needs manual work
- "Ingredients to Video" feature requires paid tier
- Basic timeline editor

## Predecessor Tools Merged In (Feb 2026)
- Whisk (visual collage/mood board) — migrated into Flow
- ImageFX (text-to-image) — migrated into Flow
- Whisk libraries deleted Apr 30, 2026 for non-opted-in users
